Introduction to Robotics:
Robotics is a multidisciplinary field that involves the design, construction, operation, and use of robots. A robot is an intelligent, programmable machine capable of carrying out tasks autonomously or semi-autonomously. Robotics combines elements from various domains such as mechanical engineering, electrical engineering, computer science, and artificial intelligence. Robotics using Arduino, Motor Shield, and Gear Motors:
In the context of creating simple robotic systems, Arduino, Motor Shields, and Gear Motors are popular components used by hobbyists and beginners. Arduino is an open-source electronics platform based on easy-to-use hardware and software, making it accessible for those new to electronics and programming.
1. Arduino:
Arduino serves as the brain of the robot, providing a platform for programming and controlling various components. It comes with an integrated development environment (IDE) that allows users to write and upload code to the Arduino board.
2. Motor Shield:
A Motor Shield is an expansion board that attaches to an Arduino board, providing the necessary circuitry to control motors. It simplifies motor control and allows the Arduino to command the movement of motors without the need for complex wiring. Motor Shields often support various types of motors, including DC motors and servo motors.
3. Gear Motors:
Gear Motors are specialized types of motors equipped with gears to achieve specific torque and speed requirements. These motors are commonly used in robotics for their ability to provide precise control over movement. The gears help in increasing torque and reducing the speed of the motor, making them suitable for applications such as driving wheels in a robot.
